Why now — three thresholds crossed in 2025

1 · MODEL CAPABILITY

Claude Sonnet 4.5 closed the unstructured-request gap

Tool use + structured outputs + 200K context = an LLM that can qualify a real service request, parse it into pricing variables, and route. Pre-2025 LLMs failed at any of these alone. Today one model holds the full flow.
2 · DISTRIBUTION

Chat platforms became the OS for services

Telegram bot API matured. WhatsApp Business + Cloud API. Instagram DM API. Native payments inside chat (Telegram Stars, WA Pay). Customers don't want apps for services anymore — they want an agent in the chat they already live in.
3 · ECONOMICS

Inference cost dropped >10× in 18 months

Our marginal cost per turn = $0.06. Per active client per month = ~$0.40. Prompt caching takes another 90% off repeat-context calls. For the first time, an AI agent doing real services work is cheaper than a $5/hr SDR.
The big picture: AI is automating white-collar work end-to-end — code, design, copy, accounting, legal. But for the next decade, AI won't profitably walk a dog, tutor a child, fix a sink, cut hair, or care for an elderly parent. Physical human services stay human-delivered — AI's role is to orchestrate the work, not perform it. Sequoia: "$13T US labor market vs. ~$300B SaaS. The next $1T company sells the work itself." We're the routing/qualification/payment layer that takes software margin on top of human delivery.
Why us — real-world integration, not API resale: Most "AI startups" are tech founders selling Claude wrappers as SaaS to businesses they've never operated. We're the inverse — a real-world experienced services business (2 years, $200K+ GMV, 1,700+ paid orders, 712 customers) integrating AI into our own ops first, then expanding to capture adjacent verticals. Naval: "Leverage goes to people who understand the layer beneath the abstraction."
